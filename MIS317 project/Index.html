Where History Began

Diriyah is more than just a historical site; it is a symbol of Saudi Arabia’s resilience and unity. As the capital of the First Saudi State, it represents the foundation upon which the modern Kingdom stands.

Wander through the winding alleyways of At-Turaif, marvel at the Salwa Palace, and immerse yourself in the Najdi architecture that has withstood the test of time.

Legacy of the First State

Key Historical Milestones

Year Image Event Significance
1446 Establishment Mani' al-Muraydi founds Diriyah on the banks of Wadi Hanifa.
1727 First Saudi State Imam Muhammad bin Saud establishes the First Saudi State.
1818 Siege of Diriyah The city falls after a brave defense against Ottoman forces.
2010 UNESCO Recognition At-Turaif district is declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
